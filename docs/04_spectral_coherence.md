# Spectral Coherence

The spectral-coherence model is the organizing diagnostic layer of the
conjecture program.

It is meant to measure whether the quaternionic lift, conjugate symmetry, and
zeta functional symmetry are aligned at a point `q`.

## Functional Target

Write the functional schematically as

```text
C(q).
```

The exact definition is part of the analytic development target. A viable
functional should:

- be real-valued,
- be defined on the selected quaternionic domain,
- restrict coherently to each complex slice,
- be symmetric under the selected conjugate operation,
- identify stationary states on `Re(q)=1/2`,
- assign a detectable coherence defect to off-slice candidates.

Candidate versions of `C(q)` are tracked in
[Candidate Coherence Functionals](07_candidate_coherence_functionals.md).

## Stationary States

A stationary spectral-coherence state is a point where the first variation of
`C(q)` vanishes in the admissible directions of the model.

The conjecture predicts that nontrivial zeta zeros correspond to such stationary
states on the quaternionic critical slice.

## Relation To Classical Criteria

The functional may need to connect with classical explicit-formula or Weil-type
criteria. That connection is not merely decorative: it is the likely bridge
between a geometric picture and analytic number theory.

The required bridge should state how positivity, negativity, or coercive
behavior in the coherence model corresponds to the classical zero condition.
